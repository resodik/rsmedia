My name is Renata Sobieniak and I’m a graduate of Design studies with a specialization in Game Development. I’ve always been passionate about video games — not only as a player, but as someone deeply fascinated by the technical and artistic process of creating game worlds.

My growing interest led me to treat my thesis as an opportunity: an excuse to dive even deeper into the development pipeline while also completing my degree and opening new paths for future growth.

Building My Own Game – From Thesis to Full Production

During my studies, I created a short, playable section of a game world — you can see some of it in my portfolio. After graduating, I decided to keep going.

I successfully applied for a startup grant, which allowed me to invest in professional tools such as a motion capture suit, a resin and filament 3D printer, a new graphic tablet, and upgraded hardware.

My Gear

I'm constantly learning and exploring new workflows and technologies that improve the quality and efficiency of my work — something I deeply care about.

Currently, I’m developing my own game in Unreal Engine 5, which allows me to grow across every aspect of game creation. I'm building custom assets in Blender and implementing gameplay mechanics — creating everything from scratch to maintain full creative control. Textures and animations are next on my list, but for now, I’m following a structured, hands-on development process that lets me prototype, test, and iterate in real time.
